    By eating more plant-based foods and less meat products, you can help reduce your impact on the planet! A recent study by Nature found that Western diets high in red meat can lead to worrying levels of climate change, as well as imbalances in the world’s ecosystems and biodiversity.

    Why? Well, it’s mainly because growing plants, as opposed to breeding cattle, requires less energy, water and land, and also produces less greenhouse gas emissions and pollutants.

    The production of animal products generates high levels of greenhouse-gas emissions. In fact, cows emit the famous greenhouse gas methane when they digest food. They also require a lot of feed, which will require a lot of water, land and fertilizer.

    Yes, plants also require water, land and fertilizer, but, overall, meat products are significantly more emissions-intensive. According to the Nature study, if we all started adopting more plant-based diets, we could reduce the greenhouse gas emissions of the food system by more than half.

    If you’re worried about protein, remember that legumes like beans and lentils are excellent protein sources!
